# Storytelling — Invernada do Sol

## The Narrative Frame

The website tells a single, continuous story. It is not a menu of pages — it is a journey that moves from the outside world, through a threshold, into a world of warmth and meaning. Every section is a chapter. Every transition is a breath.

The visitor arrives as a stranger. They leave as someone who understands, at a gut level, what Invernada do Sol feels like — before they've ever stepped foot there.

---

## The Emotional Arc

The story follows a cinematic arc borrowed from cinema and architecture:

```
ARRIVE (cold, outside, searching)
    ↓
THRESHOLD (the brand announces itself)
    ↓
INVITATION (why this place, why now)
    ↓
IMMERSION (the details that seduce)
    ↓
DESIRE (the menu, the cabins, the images)
    ↓
BELONGING (testimony, community)
    ↓
ACTION (reservation — framed as a gift to oneself)
```

Each homepage section maps to one beat of this arc. Never skip a beat. Never reorder them arbitrarily.

## Section Narratives

### 1. Hero — The Arrival
The visitor lands in darkness with a single, breathing image. No navigation visible immediately. No calls to action. Just the brand name, the landscape, and a feeling.

**Emotional goal**: Create a moment of stillness. The website makes the visitor slow down.

**Copy direction**:
- Brand name: large, italic, gold gradient
- Tagline: one line, poetic, in pt-BR
- No body copy in the hero — the image speaks

**Example copy**:
```
Invernada do Sol
"Onde o fogo aquece o que o inverno esfriou."
```

### 2. About / The Story — The Threshold
Brief, intimate. Who we are, why this place exists. Told in first or second person, not corporate biography.

**Emotional goal**: The visitor should feel they've been let into something private — a secret, a story they want to be part of.

**Copy direction**:
- 2–3 short paragraphs maximum
- Tell the origin, the vision, the place — not the menu
- Use landscape and season as emotional anchors

**Example copy**:
```
"Existe um tipo de lugar que não se escolhe. Ele te encontra no fim de uma tarde de inverno, quando o cansaço é real e o fogo é necessidade."
```

### 3. Experience — The Invitation
What does Invernada do Sol offer that nowhere else does? Not features — feelings. Not amenities — moments.

**Emotional goal**: The visitor should lean forward. Something here is for them specifically.

**Copy direction**:
- 3–4 experiential pillars
- Each pillar has an icon (Lucide), a short title, and 2 sentences max
- Titles in Cormorant italic, descriptions in DM Sans light
- Focus on sensation: warmth, silence, flavor, belonging

**Pillar examples**:
- "O Fogão" — the wood-fired kitchen, ancestral cooking techniques
- "A Neve de Julho" — the experience of South Brazilian winter
- "As Cabanas" — sleeping in the cold, waking to fog
- "O Silêncio" — away from the noise, the phones, the speed

### 4. Menu — The Seduction
Dishes shown cinematically. Large photography, minimal text. The food should look like it belongs in a film still.

**Emotional goal**: Appetite. The visitor should feel hunger — not for calories, but for the experience.

**Copy direction**:
- Dish names in Cormorant
- No lengthy descriptions — 10 words maximum per dish
- Price is secondary or absent in the preview (full menu has it)
- Seasonal framing: "Cardápio de Inverno 2025"

### 5. Gallery — The Immersion
Full-bleed images. The visitor is inside the place now. This section says nothing — it shows everything.

**Emotional goal**: The visitor closes their eyes and can picture themselves there.

**Guidelines**:
- Images over text — minimal or no copy
- Varied format: portrait, landscape, square — avoid rigidity
- Warm light, steam, hands on bowls, frost on windows, fire

### 6. Cabins — The Dream
The cabins are where the story deepens from "dinner" to "escape." This section sells the overnight fantasy.

**Emotional goal**: The visitor imagines waking up there. The fantasy is complete.

**Copy direction**:
- "Não venha apenas jantar. Fique."
- Focus on the morning: fog, silence, coffee, no rush
- Cabin names should be evocative (not "Chalé 1")

### 7. Testimonials — The Belonging
Real voices. Short. Specific. The visitor should see themselves in the testimonials.

**Emotional goal**: Social permission. "If they felt that, I will too."

**Guidelines**:
- 3–5 testimonials maximum in the main flow
- Each one specific (not "great food!") — a moment, a detail
- Author name + city, no full surnames needed

**Example**:
```
"Eu entrei esperando um jantar. Saí com a sensação de que tinha voltado de uma viagem."
— Marina, Porto Alegre
```

### 8. Reservation CTA — The Gift
The final section reframes the reservation not as a transaction but as a gift — to oneself, to someone loved.

**Emotional goal**: Making a reservation feels like an act of care.

**Copy direction**:
- Never "Reserve sua mesa" without context
- Frame it: "Você merece uma noite assim"
- WhatsApp CTA feels personal — better than a form for this brand

## Writing Principles

### The Firelight Rule
Every sentence should work if read aloud by firelight. If it sounds like marketing copy, rewrite it.

### The One-Reader Rule
Write for one person, not a demographic. "Você" not "vocês". Intimate, not broadcast.

### The Silence Rule
What you leave out is as important as what you include. Short paragraphs. White space. Let the images do work.

### The Season Rule
The copy should feel seasonally appropriate. Reference cold, fog, fire, wool — not in cliché ways, but in specific sensory details.

### The Portuguese Standard
- No anglicisms where Portuguese works beautifully
- Avoid bureaucratic connectives: "através de", "no sentido de", "tendo em vista"
- Prefer: "onde", "quando", "assim", "apenas", "ainda"

---

## Content Hierarchy

```
Image (emotion first)
    → Eyebrow label (context)
        → Headline (promise)
            → Body (depth — optional, short)
                → CTA (invitation)
```

This hierarchy applies to every section. Image leads. Words follow. Action invites.
